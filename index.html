Biography

Michelle

Obama

She was the first African-American woman to serve as first lady. As first lady, Obama worked as an advocate for poverty awareness, education, nutrition, physical activity, and healthy eating. She has written three books including her The New York Times best selling memoir Becoming (2018) and The Light We Carry (2022).
Last Known Address: Washington, D.C., USA

Education

Michelle Obama attended Princeton University, where she earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Sociology with a minor in African-American Studies in 1985. Graduating cum laude, she focused her senior thesis on the experiences of Princeton-educated African Americans within their communities, titled “Princeton-Educated Blacks and the Black Community.” Following her time at Princeton, she pursued her Juris Doctor (J.D.) at Harvard Law School, graduating in 1988. At Harvard, Michelle was actively involved in advocating for diversity and inclusion, focusing on law and public service.

Employment History

Michelle began her professional career at Sidley Austin LLP, a prestigious law firm, where she worked as an associate specializing in marketing and intellectual property from 1988 to 1991. It was during this time that she met Barack Obama, whom she mentored as a summer associate. After leaving Sidley Austin, Michelle shifted her focus to public service, working as an assistant to Chicago Mayor Richard M. Daley from 1991 to 1993. In this role, she developed policies supporting economic development and education. From 1993 to 1996, she served as the Executive Director for the Chicago office of Public Allies, a nonprofit organization dedicated to developing leadership skills in young people pursuing careers in public service.

Michelle transitioned to academia, joining the University of Chicago in 1996 as the Associate Dean of Student Services, where she launched the university's first community service program. Her leadership trajectory continued at the University of Chicago Medical Center, where she served as Executive Director of Community Affairs and eventually as Vice President for Community and External Affairs from 2002 to 2008. In these roles, she focused on strengthening relationships between the medical center and the surrounding community, further cementing her reputation as a dedicated advocate for public service and community engagement.
